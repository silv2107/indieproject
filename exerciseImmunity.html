Exercise and the Immune System

Moderate and intense exercise The immune response Exercise and viral infection

Moderate exercise in the beginning of a virus infection creates the optimal immune response to fight of the infection and to create the proper immunity. This due to the smooth transition between the two phases of the immune response (i.e. virus clearence and antibody production). The smooth transition between the phases seen after moderate exercise insures the least damage to the lung cells by blocking the killer cells from excessive damage to the cells, while at the same time producing antibodies against further external virus infection. Intensive training after virus infection creates the worst condition for a proper immune response. Thus, the first phase, virus clearence, of defence is omited and the body enter directly into second phase. Consequently, the virus can spread unchecked. No training after virus infection has the tendency for an imbalance between the phase, towards the first phase (virus clearence). Thus, the tendency towards prolonged illness is higher.
